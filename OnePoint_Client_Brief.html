Client Brief · OnePoint

A Better Way to Coordinate Care and Fill Rooms

OnePoint is a voice-first care coordination platform for families and small assisted-living operators. It replaces scattered texts and spreadsheets with one shared timeline, then bridges directly into placement workflow.

Built for real-world use: mixed-tech families, low-friction access, legal-grade logs, and practical operations for single-facility teams.

Why This Matters Now

Current reality for teams

  • Care updates live in group texts, memory, and ad hoc notes.
  • Shift handoffs are inconsistent and hard to audit later.
  • Low-tech family members are often excluded from the loop.
  • Small operators are over-served by enterprise systems and under-served by consumer apps.

What OnePoint changes

  • Tap, talk, save: voice capture becomes a structured timeline entry.
  • One shared web surface for all family members, including low-tech users.
  • Timestamped, attributed exports for legal/risk documentation.
  • Placement pipeline connects to care record at move-in without duplicate data entry.

MVP Scope (What We Are Building First)

Focused execution for immediate value:

  • Voice-first logging: record and transcribe shift notes.
  • Shared timeline: everyone sees the same updates by recipient/date.
  • On-duty + handoff: clear ownership per shift.
  • Web-first access: no-install link for low-tech users.
  • Legal export: chronological, timestamped, attributed records.
  • Spreadsheet backfill: one-time import to preserve history.

Market Insight: The Gap OnePoint Owns

The market splits into two camps: family caregiver apps (easy but light on rigor) and enterprise senior-living systems (powerful but heavy and expensive). OnePoint sits in the under-served middle: practical, voice-first, low-friction, and operationally useful.

Category Examples What They Do Well Where They Miss
Family Coordination CaringBridge, Lotsa Helping Hands, Ianacare, CircleOf, CareTree Task sharing, updates, broad accessibility No strong placement pipeline, weak legal-grade record rigor, limited voice-first workflows
Senior Living CRM / Ops Aline, Yardi, Sherpa, Enquire, ECP, MatrixCare Pipeline, occupancy, enterprise compliance workflows Heavy onboarding, not designed for low-tech family access, weak "talk-and-log" simplicity
Lead Marketplaces A Place for Mom, Caring.com, SeniorAdvisor Top-of-funnel lead generation Not a continuous care record; weak continuity after move-in
OnePoint (Target) Voice-first + family + placement bridge Unified timeline from intake to ongoing care Built specifically for this blended workflow

Top 5 Strategic Advantages

1) Voice-first in real conditions

Caregivers can log while actively providing care. Minimal interaction, maximum capture fidelity.

2) Low-tech family inclusion

Simple link-based web access means no install barrier for skeptical or less technical relatives.

3) Legal-grade chronology

Every entry is attributed and timestamped, making records practical for legal/risk review.

4) Placement-to-care continuity

Prospect data transitions to resident care record without duplicate admin work.

5) Single-facility friendly economics

Positioned for owner-operators and small teams who are currently underserved by enterprise-first software.
